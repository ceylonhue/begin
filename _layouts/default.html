Why I.T? Why now?

The reason I want to study I.T. is that I relate to it easily and adapt quickly to concepts, having grown up alongside the industry. I feel although, quite advanced in terms of what the industry has achieved, the field is still quite young, and there is much more to be discovered and m any more milestones to reach.

My interest in I.T. came when my family got our first computer, and it quickly turned into my hobby and time sink. Later learning that I.T. is not just about computers and that it affects our everyday lives, I was intrigued. After learning about automation, coding, and all the while enjoying the practical benefits from innovations such as email, telephones, internet, and security systems, I knew that I.T. was the future, or as I see it, I.T. will build the future.

After high school, I worked in a call center for an insurance company. I got to see first hand the transition from the company using "claim forms" to lodge insurance claims to all claims being submitted either online by the Policy Holder or by the claims consultant over the phone. The new claims lodgement options eliminated many steps for both parties involved, and this is one of the reasons I looked into Software engineering and furthering my knowledge and I.T.

I didn't waste time, and I was able to get a position within the same company as a software tester for the new Claim Lodgement system. This new position allowed me to see the workflow and ethics in an I.T related job. While getting used to the new environment, I made acquaintances with a handful of developers. I would always pick their brains about their roles. I did this for about two years and then went to travel to Japan for a whole year.

After an eye-opening year eating ramen and sushi which I'm yet to get sick of! I'm back, and I want to pursue my passion and career once more. However, being a bit older and having less time, my options were limited. RMIT offered online courses that suit me perfectly.

I want to broaden my knowledge in the field of I.T. from this course and use this time to figure out if Software engineering is what I want to do or something similar within I.T.

Myers-Briggs test


M.B.T. is somewhat of a self-reflection test, a psychological type of personality test. The questions revolve around four primary categories, which then determine which personality you. My results showed I was an INTP type personality or nicknamed "architect," can be described as one who has 'A love of problem-solving' I agree in some ways but not others.




Emotional intelligence Test - Talentsmart.com


Emotional intelligence is how a person handles their emotions in different types of situations. How aware are they of it? How do they react to another person's feelings? The measure of a person's ability to control their emotions and not let these factors distract their focus. That's how I would explain it, but in short, the awareness and measure of how emotions affect emotions. This E.I test was the longest out of the three tests and had some questions that get you thinking philosophically and many moments of self-reflection.

I took my time doing this test, and it seems my E.I is reasonably average, maybe even slightly above. With a score of 74 out of 100. In a nutshell, my test results indicate that I'm aware of some of the things that I need to improve on, but not everything. I'm interested in finding ways to increase my E.I. score as I think it is a commonly overlooked trait for most people.




Abstract test - www.psychometricinstitute.com.au/


This multiple-choice test comprised entirely of pictures. It asks you to identify the next shape correctly that follows a sequence, after showing you the previous four configurations of the series. Mock Project - One Stop Tutor Hire

Overview
Design a system that can be a universal access point for finding Skilled tutors in your area. A system that can be a contact point between those who wish to gain knowledge and those who want to offer it. Similiar ride-sharing apps, you can find tutors in your area who have indicated they are available. Unlike ride shares, the experience with the customer is much more hands-on and interactive. The system will show what tutors available sorted by categories depending on their skills. Each tutor "profile" will have their availability displayed and as well prices. Customers can leave feedback regarding their lessons.

Motivation
The process of getting a tutor in regards to music, in particular, is quite dated and inefficient. When I was younger and looking for a guitar teacher, I tried all the usual routes like going to the local music shop, looking in the yellow pages, using the internet, asking friends. It is just frustrating.

After finding one or several, there is no way I knew who to go with or if they were any good. All I had was a word of mouth or pure luck. To be able to have a broader "community" in one where others can leave reviews and tutors can place updates. Think Uber meets Skillshare meets music.


System Features
One database - The system will be able to store all tutor information under the same method to allow for a universal point of contact for anyone seeking skilled tutors.

Multiple OS Access
The system will be cross-platform over iOS apps, android, and P.C. to allow for greater visibility and accessibility and convenience. On all platforms, you will be able to access the same database community.

User and Tutor Logins and Profiles
The system will require a login to a registered user to keep track of all information. Users and tutors will also have user profiles, just like on most mediums these days. Individual profiles allow for sharing of updates, a point of contact between the community. Tutors can also upload any videos or documents onto their profiles.

Transactions through the App
As an additional source of revenue from doing one on one skill training, tutors can offer their services and skills in the form of videos or documents on their profile.
Freedom and flexibility for the tutors only mean more convenience for the customer. In the cases, Tutors are not available, or the user does not wish to meet 'face to face', the option is still open to learning from tutors.

Rating system
Users will be able to rate their lessons, give feedback in the form of stars, which will provide tutors with a score. The rating system is one of the essential features. Besides word of mouth at the moment there is no way to know how right a person is for the job quickly, I would aim to build up a large enough database and community that this is no longer a frustration.

Easy to start
Tutors will no longer need to take out ads in papers, on Facebook, or regularly visiting their local shop.

Community Driven
Some of the hardest parts about learning a skill are the fear of failing or feeling like you have no support or whether you are even doing it correctly. Having a teacher helps with this, but sometimes it's also too daunting to let an 'expert' in that field know how you feel. The community that will build up from the app usage from people leaving feedback on tutors and commenting on videos or just chatting to each other, the community will act as a "friend" in your learning journey to help ease the times when you are doubting.

Advertisements
The app will feature ads as a source of revenue and outreach. Because I aim to include all types of skills and not just one field. Relations with all those fields would undoubtedly form, which in the future, I hope will allow for the fine-tuning of criteria within our database searches to further provide for the customer.

Skills Needed
App design , UI design, Product design, data science, User research, product mangement

Final Outcome
Finding capable people to pass on their skills will be as easy as ordering an Uber. The feeling of not being able to learn enough from that one youtube tutorial will be old news. With fast turn around response times, availability info, and tutors offline materials. There will always be an option to find the lessons you want.
